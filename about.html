About Mud Laboratory

Welcome to Mud Laboratory.

I'm Mud, an IT professional with experience in Customer Support, IT Support, Junior IT Administration, and Junior Site Reliability Engineering (SRE). Throughout my career, I've always enjoyed solving technical problems and learning new technologies. Recently, I discovered a passion for web development and decided to challenge myself by building my own website from scratch.

I'm fascinated by how websites are designed, developed, deployed, and maintained. Every lesson I learn and every project I complete brings me one step closer to becoming a professional web developer.

My goal is simple:

To become the kind of developer businesses can trust to design, build, deploy, and maintain modern websites. I may not know everything yet, but I'm committed to learning every day, improving with every project, and sharing that journey along the way. I'm currently following a structured learning journey to strengthen my web development skills, one project at a time.

Why did I create Mud Laboratory?

Mud Laboratory is my personal learning space and digital workshop. I created it to document my progress as I learn web development, experiment with new ideas, and build real projects from the ground up. Rather than waiting until I'm an expert, I chose to learn in public and share the journey. This website grows alongside my skills, with every page representing another milestone in my development.

Can I build your website today?

I'm currently focused on building my knowledge and expanding my skills through continuous practice and real-world projects. While I'm still growing as a web developer, every project I complete strengthens my ability to design, build, and maintain modern websites. My goal is simple: to reach the point where I can confidently deliver professional websites that businesses can trust.

My Vision:

My vision is to become a trusted web developer capable of designing, building, deploying, and maintaining modern websites for businesses of all sizes. Through continuous learning, hands-on projects, and real-world experience, I aim to build solutions that are reliable, user-friendly, and meaningful.
